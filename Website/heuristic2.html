User Friendly Language

Georgia State University does a pretty good job sticking with university jargon, using terms like “financial aid, faculty and staff, students, admissions etc”. This type of language is familiar with users navigating on university sites. There are a few exceptions to GSU’s user-friendly language usage. GSU uses several acronyms, and there are no explanations to what these stand for until you click into the page.

Multiple Navigation Bars

Typically, users are familiar with a main navigation bar at the top of their page. The top one is easy to miss since it is on the very top right. Eye-tracking studies have shown that the natural flow of information for most users is in a Z-shape (left, to the right, to the bottom left, then to the bottom right). Since the focus of the home page is the Georgia State University logo, users will naturally overlook the navigation bar the very top of the page. The information on the top navigation bar contains important information for students, faculty and staff, and we believe it should be located somewhere easily visible to the user, so it does not get overlooked.
