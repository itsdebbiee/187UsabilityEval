Inconsistent Navigation Bars

Different subsites of the website do not have the same navigation bars making it hard for users to understand the layout of the entire website. Navigation is inconsistent throughout the different site pages and it may throw the user off because information is not located in a specific place but rather is determined by the layout and design of the subsites.

Inconsistent Branding Across Site

The brand is not consistent across the different college’s sites - each one has their own site layout, and the user does not get a good sense that this is the site for GSU as a whole. In addition to the inconsistent layout, the Home Logo is not present throughout the site. The Admissions page does not have the home logo to return back to home page, but instead is now a tab option on the top left of the page. The site makes it difficult for users to trace their steps when navigating. Links lead to new sites or pages that does not maintain a consistent brand. It is very easy for users to get lost and not know where they are in the site. For example, on the homepage, clicking on “Admissions” or “Research” tab sends user to pages that have completely different navigation bars. Clicking on “Athletics” sends users to a different website entirely.

Not All Tabs Have Drop Down Menus

Looking at the homepage, and the tabs, the user realizes that some tabs have a very extensive drop down menu with many options and links to other subpages. Other tabs such as the “research” tab does not have a drop down menu entirely. This type of inconsistency may confuse the user, since they may expect to see a drop-down menu.

SAT Standards

When using this tool to determine the chances of admission to GSU, users will see that their SAT requirements are not consistent with how many sections the SAT is actually divided into.
