Home Page Irrelevancy

The banner page does not change and remains on a brain and behavior image. This image is linked to a research article. This article may be relevant since Georgia State is known to be a reputable public research university; however, without previous knowledge, a typical user would not know the relevancy of this picture and may be confused which page this is, because it does not give any cues that it is the home page of a university.

Subheading Dominates

Although we are on the home page for GSU, the subheader on the homepage is much more emphasized compared to the rest of the page. This attracts attention away from the fact that it’s the home page for Georgia State University, not Brain and Behavior Research. The focus should not be about Research, but about the university as a whole. This large title can be misinterpreted as the main topic of this page, and users may find it hard to identify which page they are on.

Where Am I?

When navigating through the website, there are cases where a user will stumble onto a page that is located under two tabs. This may be confusing because the user is technically located on two pages. Navigation is unclear and this may affect the user experience in a negative way. We would suggest to keep subpages under only one tab to keep everything simple and easy to find. It may be a bit overwhelming for users when information is not consolidated and organized.
