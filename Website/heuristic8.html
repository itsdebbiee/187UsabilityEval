Minimalist Design

The Georgia State University website itself is very cluttered and unorganized. The user is unable to follow how the information is presented and organized. Certain parts of the site are more emphasized than others, and it is easy to miss pieces of information such as the titles. The pictures used do not go well together and altogether, makes that section of the page look unorganized. The social media icons are also very small and squished into one section.

On the home page, there are arrows that basically act as a giant slider, but for the content of the website, not just simple images. This gets very overwhelming as there is a lot of content on each site. They are also easy to miss and add to the clutter of the page.

The pages that the arrows take us to contain different sets of information, and at times, it isn’t inherently clear what that site is about. It is overwhelming to have all these different sets of information on the home page.

Drop Down Menu

The navigation drop down menus are very cluttered and contain multiple links for sites. All this text easily blends together, and it puts a big strain on the user’s ability to sift through this information. Having dividers between the different text or even bullet points would help the user navigate through their drop down menu.
